About

Summon Mars is the voice of a life lived through chaos, clarity, and the long road back to sound. After years of creating music while battling hearing issues, these songs rise from different chapters of a personal story finally ready to be spoken out loud.

His connection to music started early, long before studios or training. As a kid, he made songs on a game console, burned homemade CDs, and sold them with more ambition than marketing sense. Writing grew with him too — notebooks full of poetry, some published, and spoken-word performances that showed what it meant to turn experience into connection.

Everything changed after a wreck left him in a coma for six days. Doctors expected the worst, but he returned with a slow thumbs-up that reshaped his sense of time, purpose, and the urgency to create. From then on, music wasn’t a dream. It was a mission.

The name Summon Mars came later, tied to a moment that felt bigger than the everyday — a pull toward a stranger, more honest world where the work could finally match the life behind it.

Based in the eastern U.S., Summon Mars steps forward with songs that hit hard, visuals on the way, and a drive to connect with anyone who feels music the way it’s meant to be felt.
